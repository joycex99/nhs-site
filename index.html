-Ayn Rand

The National Honor Society (NHS) is the nation's premier organization established to recognize outstanding high school students. More than just an honor roll, NHS serves to recognize those students who have demonstrated excellence in the areas of scholarship, leadership, service, and character. At Fairview High School, we are a chapter of over 300 strong, each committed to making a true, positive difference in the world around us.

Fairview's NHS is built upon two foundational commitments: giving back to our school, and giving back to our community. At our school, NHS works closely with both the student body and the administration, leading a tutoring marketplace for students, working to establish zero-waste events, and collaborating with service-oriented clubs to increase participation and engagement in projects. In our community, NHS has worked with everything from the homeless shelter to Pennies for Patients, medicine horse programs to the Bolder Boulder—and that was all just in the last year alone.

Most importantly, NHS values student-run projects. Fairview High School is a community of 2,200 of Colorado's most intelligent, inquisitive, and driven students. We are incredibly proud of what our members have accomplished so far, and are always looking for new, meaningful work to collaborate on. Whether you are a local library or a remote service organization, an event coordinater or a speaker looking to spread your message, rest assured that there will always be something here for you.

Our Projects

Fairview NHS students create, engage in, and pursue dozens of different projects each year, both in the local community and beyond. Below are just a few of the highlights. To see the full list, please follow the link below.

Boulder Homeless Shelter

The second and fourth Saturday of every month, NHS members are up at 5:30 serving breakfast at the Boulder Homeless Shelter.

Cyber Seniors

Paired one on one with a senior in the community, Fairview students teach them how to use basic technology.

Silent Auction

From building the website to soliciting donations to hand-delivering the items, NHS students ran a silent auction that raised >$4000 for Pennies for Patients.

Bolder Boulder

We love it as much as you do! Each year Fairview NHS is at one of the mile marker water stations, cheering on the community.

Medicine Horse Program

Once a month, NHS volunteers help care for the horses that provide psychotherapy for adolescents, families, and adults.

Ignite

Each winter, NHS students partner with Ignite Adaptive Sports to offer snow-sports lessons to people with disabilities.
